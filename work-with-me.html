Work With Me

I help mission-driven teams turn complex evidence into clear decisions. My approach is shaped by scientific research and computational systems work: define the real question, isolate what matters, and produce outputs people can actually use.

What I’m best at

I turn technically complex work into practical direction without flattening the nuance.

I focus on:

  • Translation: connecting technical analysis to the decisions leaders and teams need to make.
  • Compression: distilling large volumes of information into the few signals that change the outcome.
  • Alignment: clarifying goals, constraints, tradeoffs, and next steps so teams can move together.

Problems I can help with

  • “We have a lot of data, but no clear direction.”
  • “Our strategy feels scattered, and we need a plan we can actually execute.”
  • “We’re doing meaningful work, but we’re stuck on what to prioritize next.”
  • “We need to explain something complex to non-experts without losing the important details.”
  • “We’re moving fast and need a rigorous check on assumptions, risks, and tradeoffs.”

Ways to work together

I work in bounded engagements with clear scope and deliverables. The goal is practical momentum and reusable artifacts—not long-term dependency.

Decision Brief (1–2 pages)

A concise, structured brief that frames the decision, highlights key signals, surfaces tradeoffs, and recommends next steps.

Best for: leadership decisions, prioritization, roadmap choices, stakeholder alignment.

Learning Arc / Curriculum Outline

A sequenced outline that turns a complex topic into a clear learning pathway, with concepts layered in a logical order for reuse.

Best for: science outreach, educational initiatives, internal learning programs.

Clarity Session (60–90 minutes)

A focused working session to define the real problem, sharpen the core questions, map constraints, and decide on immediate next moves.

Best for: stalled projects, early‑stage ideas, research direction, team alignment.

How I work

  • I optimize for clarity first, then speed.
  • I use asynchronous collaboration where it increases focus and quality.
  • I prefer small, high-trust engagements with clear outcomes.
  • I am rigorous about assumptions and careful about how conclusions are communicated.
  • I commit deeply to the work I take on.

Why me

I’m a PhD researcher in Planetary Science and Astrobiology at Purdue, where I build computational pipelines and chemical models to study complex planetary environments. That work requires turning noisy raw outputs into interpretable results that can guide scientific and mission-level decisions.

Before graduate research, I worked at Epic Systems as a Technical Solutions Engineer, helping teams navigate high-stakes operational systems where clarity and reliability matter. Across both worlds, the throughline is the same: I make complex systems legible so people can make better decisions with confidence.
